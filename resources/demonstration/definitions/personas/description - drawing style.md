MASTER STYLE DESCRIPTION — CONSISTENT PERSONA ILLUSTRATION

Create the image as a semi-realistic professional editorial portrait illustration for a healthcare, research, or innovation presentation. The result must look clearly illustrated rather than photographed, while still feeling highly credible, human, polished, and presentation-safe. The visual style should sit between photography and illustration: realistic in anatomy, expression, and proportions, but softened and refined so it is unmistakably a fictional persona portrait rather than a real person’s photo.

STYLE GOAL
The portrait should communicate professionalism, trustworthiness, intelligence, calmness, and warmth. It should feel suitable for a serious stakeholder presentation in healthcare, research, data stewardship, interoperability, or digital innovation. The overall impression should be modern, clean, credible, and understated.

LEVEL OF REALISM
Use soft realism, not photorealism. Preserve believable human anatomy, realistic facial proportions, and natural posture. The rendering should be detailed enough to support identity consistency, but not so literal that it resembles a stock photo or creates uncanny AI realism. The person must look like a refined illustrated character grounded in reality.

FACE RENDERING
Render the face with clean, controlled detail and natural proportions. Facial structure should be clear and stable, including forehead, cheeks, jawline, nose, lips, chin, and eye placement. Use subtle tonal modeling to define form. Skin should look natural and softly textured, never plastic, glossy, hyper-retouched, or overly detailed. Fine lines and skin variation may be lightly suggested, but should remain elegant and restrained. Expression should be calm, professional, approachable, and slightly warm, typically with a subtle confident smile or composed neutral expression.

EYES
The eyes should be natural, expressive, and believable, with clear irises, soft detail, and realistic catchlights. They should feel attentive and intelligent without becoming dramatic, glossy, oversized, or stylized. Avoid exaggerated contrast, fantasy-like brightness, or cinematic intensity.

HAIR
Hair should be illustrated with believable volume, shape, direction, and softness. Show grouped strands and gentle texture rather than hyper-detailed individual hairs. The hairstyle should feel neat, natural, and professional. Avoid windblown motion, fashion styling, excessive gloss, or decorative effects. Hair must remain consistent for the same persona across different images.

LINEWORK
Use minimal or invisible linework. Forms should be defined mainly through value, color, and soft edge control rather than outlines. Do not use comic lines, sketch marks, visible brush contours, inked edges, or cartoon-style strokes.

LIGHTING
Use soft, even, flattering portrait lighting. The preferred effect is diffused daylight or gentle office lighting. Shadows should be subtle, smooth, and realistic, never harsh or dramatic. The face should be clearly visible and evenly modeled. Avoid cinematic contrast, spotlight effects, strong rim light, colored light, or theatrical mood lighting.

COLOR PALETTE
Use muted, modern, natural colors suited to healthcare and research presentations. Favor soft blues, gentle grays, off-whites, muted greens, restrained earth tones, and natural skin tones. Keep saturation moderate to low. The image should feel clean and calm, not vivid, trendy, or stylized through color grading. Avoid neon tones, heavy orange-teal grading, social-media filters, or overly warm or cool tints.

SHADING AND TEXTURE
Use smooth, subtle shading with gentle gradients and refined transitions. Add enough depth to give the face, hair, and clothing convincing volume, but keep the rendering clean and restrained. Texture should be selective and understated. The finish should feel crisp, premium, and digitally illustrated, without rough painterly brushwork, watercolor effects, oil painting texture, cel shading, or plastic 3D rendering.

CLOTHING
Clothing should be professional, quiet, and realistic. Use understated business-casual or academic-professional attire such as blazers, cardigans, collared shirts, blouses, or fine-knit layers. Fabrics should be suggested through simple folds and subtle shading. Avoid logos, loud patterns, high-fashion styling, shiny materials, flashy accessories, or anything that competes with the face.

COMPOSITION
Use a portrait layout appropriate for persona cards and slide decks. Frame the subject as head-and-shoulders or chest-up. Maintain a stable, balanced composition with an upright, relaxed, confident posture. The person may face the viewer directly or at a slight angle, but the pose should always feel natural and composed. Avoid dynamic action poses, extreme crops, dramatic camera angles, or exaggerated body language.

BACKGROUND
Use a clean, softly blurred, simplified office, research, or healthcare-innovation environment. The background should suggest a credible professional setting without drawing attention away from the subject. Include only subtle environmental cues such as desks, glass walls, shelves, monitors, meeting rooms, or abstracted architectural shapes. Keep detail low and visual contrast restrained. Avoid clutter, busy scenes, scenic landscapes, highly specific locations, or decorative graphic backgrounds.

VISUAL CONSISTENCY RULES
All persona portraits in the set must share the same visual language:

- same level of realism
- same lighting logic
- same color restraint
- same rendering quality
- same background treatment
- same professional tone
- same portrait framing logic

When regenerating the same persona, preserve the same identity cues and visual treatment. Only scene-dependent details such as crop, pose, outfit color, or background variation may change. The image should always look like the same person illustrated in the same series.

INTENDED EFFECT
The portrait must immediately read as a polished fictional persona illustration for a serious professional context. It should feel human and relatable, but clearly not like a real staff photo or stock image. The viewer should perceive it as credible, consistent, and thoughtfully designed for a healthcare or research presentation.

NEGATIVE CONSTRAINTS
Do not make it photorealistic.
Do not make it cartoonish.
Do not make it flat vector art.
Do not make it comic-book style.
Do not make it anime.
Do not make it painterly.
Do not make it highly textured.
Do not make it cinematic.
Do not make it glossy stock-photo style.
Do not make it fashion editorial.
Do not make it exaggerated or caricature-like.
Do not make it uncanny or overly AI-looking.

SHORT MASTER STYLE FORMULA
Semi-realistic professional editorial portrait illustration, soft realism, believable anatomy, clean facial detail, subtle shading, muted natural colors, minimal linework, calm professional expression, polished healthcare and research presentation aesthetic, softly blurred office or innovation background, clearly illustrated but highly credible, modern, trustworthy, and consistent across a persona series.
