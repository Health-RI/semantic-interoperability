# REPRODUCIBLE STYLE DESCRIPTION — PROFESSIONAL PERSONA PORTRAIT ILLUSTRATION

## Overall visual identity

A polished, semi-realistic digital portrait-illustration style for professional healthcare, research, interoperability, policy, data, and innovation presentations. The style is clearly illustrated rather than photographic, yet remains highly credible, human, refined, calm, and presentation-safe.

The subject reads as a fictional but believable professional persona, not as a real staff photo, not as a stock-photo portrait, and not as a stylized character. The visual language sits precisely between photography and digital illustration: more realistic than flat illustration, less literal than a photograph, more polished than sketch art, more controlled than painterly portraiture, and warmer and more human than generic corporate imagery.

## Realism level

The realism level is soft realism only. Human anatomy, posture, proportions, and facial construction remain natural and believable. The style does not use photorealistic micro-detail, cinematic realism, exaggerated beauty retouching, or uncanny AI sharpness. Facial proportions are not stylized, features are not exaggerated, and the overall appearance is neither cartoonish nor fashion-editorial.

## Rendering medium and finish

The rendering medium is a clean digital illustration with a soft matte finish, smooth tonal gradations, restrained brush visibility, almost invisible linework, and subtle painted modeling. The result feels premium, crisp, and presentation-ready, while remaining unmistakably illustrated.

Forms are defined primarily through value, color temperature, soft tonal separation, and edge control rather than through outlines. Ink lines, comic contours, sketch marks, cel shading, and visible brush strokes are absent. Surface treatment remains matte and softly diffused across skin, hair, fabric, and background. Gloss, HDR-like sheen, plastic smoothness, and overly polished photographic sharpness are outside the style.

## Facial structure and skin treatment

Facial construction is stable and realistic, including forehead, cheeks, jawline, chin, nose, lips, and eye spacing. The face is softly modeled, with restrained but readable depth and plane definition. Skin appears natural, gently textured, and lightly varied, but never glossy, plastic, over-smoothed, or overly detailed. Fine age lines or minor skin variation may be lightly suggested when appropriate, always in an elegant and restrained way.

Expressions communicate calmness, trustworthiness, intelligence, competence, and warmth. The most typical expression is a subtle closed-mouth smile or a composed neutral-friendly expression. Exaggerated emotion, broad grin, dramatic seriousness, and model-like attitude are not characteristic of the style.

## Eyes

The eyes are realistic, attentive, and softly expressive. Iris detail is natural, catchlights are small and believable, contrast is restrained, eyelid definition is soft, and the whites of the eyes remain neutral. The style does not include oversized eyes, glamor sparkle, fantasy brightness, cinematic intensity, or overly sharpened contrast.

## Hair

Hair is neatly groomed, professional, and naturally volumetric. It is rendered as grouped forms and soft strand clusters rather than as individual hyper-detailed hairs. Direction, silhouette, and mass remain believable; highlights are restrained; the finish is matte to softly satin-like, never glossy. Excessive shine, windblown motion, decorative flyaways, salon-glam treatment, and hair-by-hair realism are absent. For the same persona across multiple generations, the hairstyle, silhouette, and overall hair character remain visually consistent.

## Lighting

Lighting is soft, diffuse, neutral, and flattering, comparable to bright indirect daylight or calm office ambient light. Portrait lighting is frontal to three-quarter, with fully readable facial features and shallow, soft shadows. Contrast stays low, and transitions across skin, hair, and clothing remain smooth.

Dramatic side light, hard cast shadows, spotlight effects, colored light, rim light, backlight, moody cinematic lighting, and theatrical contrast are not part of the style.

## Color palette

The palette is restrained, muted, and presentation-safe. Typical tones include navy, slate blue, cool gray, warm gray, off-white, soft beige, subtle desaturated greens, and natural skin tones. Saturation remains low to moderate; contrast remains moderate and clean. The overall color impression is calm, bright, modern, and understated.

Neon colors, strong orange-teal grading, trendy filters, heavy warm or cool casts, and dramatic color separation are outside the style.

## Clothing

Clothing is understated, realistic, and appropriate for healthcare, academia, research, policy, or institutional contexts. Typical garments include navy or charcoal blazers, soft blue collared shirts, white blouses, fine-knit layers, and similarly minimal business-casual or academic-professional attire. Clothing supports the portrait rather than competing with it. Fabric folds and shading remain subtle and controlled.

Logos, loud patterns, glossy fabrics, fashion-forward styling, statement accessories, and visually dominant garments are not characteristic of the style.

## Composition and camera logic

Composition is chest-up or head-and-shoulders, with upright, relaxed, confident posture. The camera angle is neutral and presentation-friendly, approximately at eye level. The subject may appear front-facing, near-profile, side-profile, or in three-quarter view, but the pose always feels composed, balanced, and natural.

Dynamic gestures, action poses, dramatic cropping, tilted framing, extreme close-ups, wide-angle distortion, low-angle hero shots, and theatrical body language are outside the style.

## Background treatment

The background is a softly blurred, low-detail office, research, healthcare, or innovation interior. It suggests a credible professional environment without becoming specific or distracting. Typical cues include large windows, glass partitions, desks, meeting-room shapes, monitors, shelving, and abstracted architectural forms.

The background remains bright, desaturated, low-contrast, and visually quiet. Readable text, branding, clutter, strong narrative elements, dramatic scenery, outdoor landscapes, and highly specific equipment are absent.

## Edge treatment

A subtle feathered edge treatment surrounds the portrait area, especially near the lower and outer edges, so the figure gently dissolves into a light background or white canvas. The effect resembles a refined masked wash or soft presentation-style fade rather than a hard crop or expressive paint splash. The fade remains elegant, controlled, understated, and consistent across the series.

## Persona-sheet format

When the style is used as a persona sheet rather than as a single portrait, the format consists of exactly three coordinated bust portraits of the same fictional person in one horizontal composition: one front-facing view, one side-profile or near-profile view, and one front-facing or three-quarter view with a slightly warmer expression.

All three views depict the same person consistently, with matching identity cues, facial structure, hairstyle family, clothing family, lighting logic, background treatment, realism level, and rendering finish. The portraits are evenly spaced on a clean, light background. The persona’s name appears centered underneath in a simple medium-gray sans-serif font, clean and understated, suitable for a presentation slide. Typography remains secondary and neutral.

## Series consistency

Across the entire portrait series, the realism level, rendering finish, lighting logic, color restraint, background softness, edge-fade treatment, professional tone, and overall presentation polish remain identical. Between personas, only person-specific traits change, such as facial features, age cues, hair color, hairstyle, outfit variation, or subtle expression nuance.

The medium, finish, mood, and illustration logic remain fixed. The full series reads as one unified set created by the same illustrator for the same institutional presentation.

## Intended impression

The overall impression is one of trust, competence, warmth, calm, intelligence, and credibility. The style is suitable for stakeholder presentations, healthcare or research strategy decks, interoperability or data-stewardship communications, policy-facing materials, and institutional storytelling. The final result is clearly illustrated but highly believable, polished, modern, and understated.

## Negative style boundaries

The style is not photorealistic, not cartoonish, not anime, not comic-book style, not flat vector art, not painterly or expressive, not oil-painting-like, not watercolor-like, not glossy, not cinematic, not dramatic, not high-fashion, not stock-photo realistic, not uncanny, not heavily textured, not hyper-detailed, not strongly outlined, not harshly shadowed, not background-heavy, not exaggerated in smiles or poses, and not visibly AI-artifacted, asymmetrical, duplicated, warped, or inconsistent across views.

## Style lock

The rendering style, lighting softness, background blur, matte finish, muted palette, feathered edge fade, restrained professionalism, and semi-realistic editorial illustration language remain fixed across all future generations. Only person-specific attributes change; the visual style itself does not.
